## Example: Content Strategy (Solo Creator)

```markdown
# Content Strategy

## Platforms
| Platform | Audience | Topics | Cadence | Skill File? |
|----------|----------|--------|---------|-------------|
| LinkedIn | Marketing leaders at B2B SaaS startups (Series A-C) | Content strategy, attribution, anti-fluff takes | 3-4x/week | Yes — `linkedin-post.md` |
| Newsletter ("The Brief") | Same as LinkedIn + deeper practitioners | Deep dives on one content strategy topic | Monthly | No |
| X/Twitter | Broader marketing/startup community | Hot takes, threads on what works vs. what people think works | 1-2x/week | No |

## Content Pillars
1. **Anti-volume content strategy**: Most companies optimize for output. I optimize for pipeline. Every piece should be traceable to revenue.
2. **Attribution reality**: What actually works for measuring content ROI vs. the tools and methods people pretend work.
3. **Practitioner stories**: Real case studies from client work — what we tried, what happened, what I'd do differently.

## Workflow
1. **Ideas**: Capture in Apple Notes throughout the week. Usually sparked by client conversations or something wrong I see on LinkedIn.
2. **Draft**: Write first draft in Claude — usually start with "here's the idea, draft a LinkedIn post" or "expand this into a newsletter piece."
3. **Edit**: I always rewrite the opening and closing. Claude gets the middle 70% right but the bookends need my voice.
4. **Publish**: LinkedIn directly. Newsletter through ConvertKit. X through the app.
5. **Where Claude helps most**: First drafts, research for newsletter pieces, repurposing (turning a newsletter into 3-4 LinkedIn posts).

## Current Content Queue
- Newsletter: "Why Your Content Calendar Is Holding You Back" — draft due March 15
- LinkedIn series: 5-part thread on attribution myths — outlines done, drafting this week
- Client case study: SaaS company that cut content volume 60% and grew pipeline — waiting on client approval to publish

## Last Updated
March 2026
```

## What Makes These Good

1. **Content strategy captures workflow, not just platforms.** "Where Claude helps most" is the most actionable section — it tells future Claude sessions exactly where to insert themselves.
2. **Team file includes working notes.** "Don't send her walls of text — bullets only" is the kind of detail that makes Claude actually useful in context.
3. **Communication rules are specific and institutional.** "CC Priya on any vendor commitment over $5K" — these are the rules people usually keep in their heads and forget to share.
4. **Project files have honest current state.** Not "everything is on track" — real status with real blockers.
5. **Key links are included.** A project file without links to the actual work is just a description. The links make it a reference.
6. **Milestones mix done and undone.** Checking off completed milestones shows progress and gives Claude context on what's been accomplished.
